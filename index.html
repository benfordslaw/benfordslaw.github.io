Zydeco (2019)

  • Selected Writings

    In the first months of 2019, Gabriel Lepak and I began an effort to disrupt the monotony of submissions to Sisyphus, the school's literature and art magazine for which I worked as an editor.
    With the eventual help of a few other classmates we generated over 600 works in the span of half a year, archiving and distributing along the way.
    Many of these works were concerned with the St. Louis counties and the people living in them. Naturally, we began to engage critically with whiteness through poetry.

    1. how could you not join the boy scouts?
my brother and i sank to the knees
in mud water
and the truck sank to the rims
in fish(i sank in fish        too
                  being the younger sibling
                      i hate to see their eyes)
that was after he burnt off my eyebrows
look in this hole
he said     having dug a hole
and up it went(and up i went
                    and up went its spine
  i buried the fish in the sand for that one)
          MIKE M. 3:32-45
      
    2. i made eggs and threw them away yesterday
they went pan to can, they really did
i guess i just like to watch the edges brown and coil
and i like the bubbles that sink
i think they were expired anyway
but i didn’t try that water trick i saw on facebook

maybe if i did i’d know
but you can’t believe everything you see
especially on the internet
and even if i did it wouldn’t matter

because the kids rolled the bins down this morning
kids need the discipline, you know
and i can’t wait for a war to teach it to them

medicine helps,
but tom’s a businessman
they leave science to scientists
he just makes it look good
so we brush our hair and teeth in this house
it’s all right on the fridge

you know we go through a gallon of milk every week?

            KATHY D. 4:1-20

Turner's

  • Milk

    In this 4 minute film shot on my parents' home video camcorder, Benji K. purchases a carton of Turner's whole milk and bring it home only to become the milk itself
    This project is in dialogue with whiteness related to identity, advertisement, nostalgia, and childhood.
    The music is recorded on a tape deck with the erase-head removed.
    Filmed in 2020 on my 19th birthday.
